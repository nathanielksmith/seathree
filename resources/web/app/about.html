SeaThree

About

SeaThree scrapes live the Twitter feeds of activists, journalists, citizens and officials from Spanish and English speakers in North and South America and translates it in real time. We have curated a select "list of lists" after discussing with many people about what accounts would be important to follow.

Why?

We at SeaThree wondered why the Internet&emdash;with such a global architecture&emdash;has remained linguistically and geographically divided. What valuable voices are lost across state lines and local languages? Being based in California, we are aware of how our border with Mexico has become increasinly militarized. Journalists are tragically frequent targets of violence in Mexico and our American media continually demonizes the Mexican drug war. As walls are raised while trade laws are relaxed, it is imperative to hear the voices on the ground in order to build a more fair and aware relationship between Mexico and the United States. SeaThree is a tiny but important step in that direction.

Operating Costs

SeaThree uses Google's Translation API, which costs $20 for every 1,000,000 characters translated. We are hosted on Rackspace and spend around $11 a month on hosting. We accept donations via Paypal, Bitcoin, and Dogecoin and rely on them to keep the service going.
